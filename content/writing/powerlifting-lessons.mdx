## Introduction

Powerlifting and design share more in common than you might think. The lessons learned under the barbell translate surprisingly well to design practice.

## Iterate Through Trial and Error

Just as lifting requires constant adjustment of form and technique, design demands iterative refinement based on feedback and results.

## Focus on Details with Clear Vision

Small adjustments in form can make the difference between a successful lift and a failed one. The same precision applies to design decisions.

## Ditch Ego

The weight doesn't care about your feelings. Both lifting and design require humility to acknowledge mistakes and learn from them.

## Stay Consistent

Progress in both domains comes from consistent practice over time, not sporadic bursts of effort.

## Value Teamwork

Even individual sports benefit from coaching, support, and community. Design is similarly collaborative.

## Build Resilience

Failure is inevitable in both lifting and design. What matters is how you respond and what you learn from setbacks.
